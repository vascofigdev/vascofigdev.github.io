About me

Hi! My name is Vasco Figueiredo, and I'm a software developer from Braga, Portugal. From a very young age, I've always loved playing video games and dreamed of one day making my own. This inspired me to start learning how to code, which sparked my interest in programming and led me to become a software developer. Currently, I'm finishing my Computer Science master's thesis, where I'm working on a real-time Wind Simulation, using Fluid Dynamics.

My current areas of interest are low-level programming, video game development and computer graphics. I consider myself a hardworking, goal-oriented person, and I enjoy working in a team environment. I love learning new concepts and improving my skills, so I'm always open to challenging myself and trying new things.

Education

Master’s Degree in Computer Science

University of Minho | 2019 - present

The master is focused on two specializations:
- Computer Graphics, where I studied the rendering pipeline, implemented different rendering algorithms (such as ray tracing), learned how to work with CUDA and how to write shaders. Additionally, I gained experience optimizing applications using the GPU.
- Parallel and Distributed Computing, where I studied the CPU and GPU architectures, and learned how to effectively improve, analyze and profile the performance and scalability of different algorithms and applications. The optimizations were achieved through multithreading methods that take advantage of hardware features, such as multiple cores, SIMD architectures or by utilizing multi-CPU machines.

I'm currently working on my master's thesis, which aims to compare the perfomance and scalability of two different fluid dynamic methods, in order to realistically simulate wind in real time. This work is being developed using a self-made engine written in OpenGL and C++.

Bachelor’s Degree in Digital Games Development Engineering

Polytechnic Institute of Cávado and Ave | 2016 - 2019

This bachelor degree is a combination of Computer Science and Game Development. I learned the basics of software engineering, such as software analysis and development, computational mathematics, algorithms and data structures, data storage, programming paradigms and network programming. I also learned game development focused subjects such as game development techniques, computer graphics, 3D programming, and artificial intelligence applied to games.
